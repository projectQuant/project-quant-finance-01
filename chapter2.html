Chapter 2: Financial Markets Overview

What Are Financial Markets?

Financial markets are platforms where buyers and sellers trade financial instruments like stocks, bonds, currencies, and derivatives. These markets help raise capital, transfer risk, and manage wealth efficiently.

Types of Financial Markets

  • Equity Market: Where companies raise money by selling shares (e.g., NSE, NYSE).
  • Debt Market: Where governments or firms borrow money by issuing bonds.
  • Derivatives Market: Contracts based on the value of other assets (e.g., options, futures).
  • Forex Market: The global market for trading currencies.

How Do Quants Use These Markets?

Quants build models that forecast prices, measure risk, and automate trading in these markets. For example:

  • In the stock market, they predict trends using past data.
  • In forex, they detect patterns in exchange rate fluctuations.
  • In derivatives, they calculate fair prices using math (e.g., Black-Scholes model).

Real-Life Example

A quantitative trading firm uses AI to analyze 10 years of market data. It discovers that the bond market tends to react 3 hours after a currency shock. It uses this to create an automated trading strategy.
